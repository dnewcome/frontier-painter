# Frontier Painter ‚Äî Slice "Magnetic Boots" ‚Äî Official Demo

This is the official playthrough demo of the **magnetic-boots** slice. A player
who normally free-floats in zero-g can now **engage magnetic boots** to plant on
any of the room's 6 interior surfaces and **walk** them ‚Äî crossing the 90¬∞ edges
to traverse **floor ‚Üí wall ‚Üí ceiling** ‚Äî then **push off** back into zero-g
float, **draw a handhold**, and **pull hand-over-hand to the goal** to win. The
existing draw ‚Üí grab ‚Üí pull ‚Üí goal ‚Üí win loop is preserved verbatim and exercised
as the finale.

- Run label: `slice-magboots`
- Generated by: `RUN_LABEL=slice-magboots npm run playthrough`
- Capture: headless Chromium (Playwright) driving the deterministic `window.game`
  automation API directly (no synthetic pointer/keyboard input), recording a
  webm and one labeled screenshot per beat, then transcoding to `demo.mp4` /
  `demo.gif` with ffmpeg.
- **Result: `goalReached === true`** (and the boots traversal verified to
  reproduce exactly ‚Äî see beat 12).

## Scene legend (what you see on screen)

- **Blue avatar figure** ‚Äî the player, drawn in third-person as a small,
  surface-oriented humanoid: a **blue** capsule body with a light-blue sphere
  head, a bright visor on the front of the head marking which way it faces, and
  two warm-orange **boots** at the feet. It is purely cosmetic and follows the
  deterministic player pose every rendered frame, so it stands upright on the
  floor, sideways out of a wall, and **head-down on the ceiling** ‚Äî the
  magnetic-boots fantasy made visible. The actual physics body remains the
  invisible collision ellipsoid `[0.4, 0.4, 0.4]`. Spawns floating at
  `[0, 1, -8]`. The avatar is hidden in the first-person camera (beat 06) so it
  doesn't fill the cockpit view.
- **Magnetic boots** ‚Äî when engaged the player plants on the nearest surface
  (its inward normal becomes "up", height snapped to `standHeight = 1.0 m`) and
  walks the tangent plane at `walkSpeed = 3.0 m/s`, unfolding across each 90¬∞
  edge onto the adjacent surface. The HUD shows `boots: ON  surface‚Üë: <normal>`.
- **Green emissive sphere** ‚Äî the goal at `[0, 1, 8]`; glows green while idle and
  turns **gold** when reached. Win triggers within `goalRadius = 1.0 m`.
- **Amber/orange tube** ‚Äî a frozen handhold created from a drawn stroke
  (`handholdRadius = 0.12 m`).
- **HUD (top-left text)** ‚Äî live `getState()` mirror: ready, position, velocity,
  grabbing/grabT, boots on/off + surface normal + facing, handhold count,
  distance-to-goal, elapsed sim time. The green **"GOAL REACHED ‚Äî WIN!"** banner
  appears on win.

The surface-walking beats are filmed from the **tracking `demo` camera** (it
follows the player at a fixed oblique offset clamped inside the sealed box) so
the floor ‚Üí wall ‚Üí ceiling path reads clearly, and the **blue avatar figure** is
visible standing/walking on the floor (beat 03), climbing the wall (beat 04), and
hanging head-down from the ceiling (beat 05). Beat 06 switches to the
**first-person `fp` camera** so the embodied, surface-relative view is visible:
while planted on the ceiling the camera up is `[0, -1, 0]` (the room appears
inverted) and the avatar is hidden so it doesn't fill the cockpit.
